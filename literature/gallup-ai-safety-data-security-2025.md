# Gallup Survey: Americans Prioritize AI Safety and Data Security (2025)

## Overall Safety Priority

### Safety Over Speed

- **80% of U.S. adults** believe "the government should maintain rules for AI safety and data security, even if it means developing AI capabilities more slowly"
- **Only 9%** prioritize rapid development over safety measures
- **11%** are unsure

### Cross-Party Consensus

Strong bipartisan support for safety prioritization:
- **88% of Democrats** back safety rules
- **79% of Republicans and Independents** support safety rules

This represents rare unified agreement on technology policy across political lines.

## Trust in AI Systems

### Overall Trust Levels

- **2%** "fully" trust AI's capability to make unbiased decisions
- **29%** trust it "somewhat"
- **69%** distrust AI either somewhat (40%) or fully (20%)

### Trust Among AI Users

- **46%** of active AI users express some level of trust/confidence
- **Non-users** show significantly lower confidence levels
- **Familiarity effect**: People who use AI tend to have more confidence

## Harm-Benefit Perception

### Overall Assessment

- **56%** believe AI has neutral effects (equal harm and good)
- **31%** think AI does more harm than good (down from 40% in previous year)
- **13%** believe good outweighs harms

## Governance and Regulatory Preferences

### Regulatory Approach

Americans favor multiple oversight mechanisms:

- **54%** support government regulation of private companies
- **53%** prefer industry-created shared standards
- **16%** believe each company should self-regulate

### Testing and Evaluation Standards

- **72%** want independent experts conducting safety evaluations
- **48%** support government-led safety testing
- **37%** support company-led testing

*Strong preference for third-party, independent evaluation over self-regulation or government-only approaches.*

## Government Role in AI Safety

### National Security Authority

- **96%** say the government should handle potential AI-related national security threats
- **62%** believe the government (not businesses) should take the lead
- **Only 34%** support business-led national security oversight

### Responsibility Across Sectors

Americans believe government should regulate AI use in:
- Hiring decisions
- Healthcare recommendations
- Autonomous vehicles
- Electoral information

## International Cooperation

### Multilateral Development Preferences

- **42%** prefer collaboration with a broad coalition of allies
- **19%** support partnerships with smaller ally groups
- **14%** prefer independent U.S. development

### Political Party Differences

- Democrats show stronger preference for international cooperation (58%)
- Republicans less enthusiastic about multilateral approaches (30%)
- Both groups favor cooperation over isolation

## Data Privacy and Security Concerns

### Specific Data Protection Priorities

Americans believe government responsibility for:
- **Spread of false information (AI-amplified misinformation)**: 88%
- **Personal data privacy violations**: 82%
- **Unauthorized use of individuals' likeness**: 81%
- **Job displacement from AI**: 68%

### Business Responsibility for Safety Harms

- **84%** say businesses are mostly (32%) or equally (52%) responsible for safety harms
- **68%** say government is mostly (16%) or equally (52%) responsible
- **52%** believe both government and business share equal responsibility

## Job Displacement Concerns

### Employment Impact Expectations

- **75%** expect AI will reduce job opportunities over the next decade
- **57%** identify transparency as the most effective strategy to reduce public concerns

### Responsibility for Addressing Job Loss

- **32%** say businesses are mostly responsible for addressing job displacement
- **52%** believe government and business share responsibility equally
- **16%** say government is mostly responsible

## Knowledge and Understanding

### AI Literacy

- **64%** report being at least somewhat knowledgeable about AI
- **9%** claim extreme/comprehensive knowledge
- **36%** admit limited or no knowledge

### Demographics of Knowledge

- **Gender gap**: 72% of men vs. 57% of women report being knowledgeable
- **Age gap**: Significant decline in knowledge for adults 60+

## Specific Application Concerns

Americans expressing concern about AI use in:

- **Hiring decisions**: 85%
- **Autonomous vehicles**: 83%
- **Medical recommendations**: 80%
- **Student homework assistance**: 67%
- **Political decision-making**: 58%
- **News media coverage**: 53%

## Business Responsibility for Transparency

### Transparency as Trust-Building

- **57%** identify transparency as the most effective strategy businesses can adopt
- Creates direct mechanism for reducing public anxiety about AI implementation
