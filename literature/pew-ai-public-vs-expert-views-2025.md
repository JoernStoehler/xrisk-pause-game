# Pew Research Center: US Public vs AI Expert Views on Artificial Intelligence (2025)

## Executive Summary

A major gap exists between how AI experts and the American public view artificial intelligence. Experts are significantly more optimistic about AI's future impact than the general public, while both groups express concerns about regulation and personal control over AI.

## Overall Sentiment and Outlook

### Public vs Expert Optimism

- **AI experts**: 56% believe AI will have a very or somewhat positive impact on the United States over the next 20 years
- **American public**: Only 17% hold this optimistic view
- **Inverse sentiment**: 51% of U.S. adults express more concern than excitement about increased AI use, compared to just 15% of experts
- **Expert excitement**: 47% of experts are more excited than concerned about increased AI use

### Concern Trajectories

- **Public concern growth**: 50% of Americans say they're more concerned than excited about AI (up from 37% in 2021)
- **Risk perception**: 57% of the public rates societal risks of AI as high
- **Benefit perception**: Only 25% of the public rate benefits of AI as high
- **Expert confidence**: 67% of experts view benefits as equal to or exceeding risks

## Specific Concerns

### Job and Employment Fears

- **Public**: 56% extremely or very concerned about AI eliminating jobs
- **Experts**: 25% express similar concern
- **Trust gap**: 75% of Americans believe AI will reduce job opportunities over the next decade

### Human Connection and Social Impact

- **57% of the public** is highly concerned about AI leading to less connection between people
- **Healthcare concerns**: 80% concerned about AI in medical recommendations
- **Hiring decisions**: 85% concerned about AI in hiring decisions
- **Autonomous vehicles**: 83% concerned about AI-driven vehicles

### Specific Application Worries

Americans express high concern about AI use in:
- Hiring decisions: 85%
- Autonomous vehicle operation: 83%
- Medical recommendations: 80%
- Political decision-making: 58%
- News media coverage: 53%

## Regulation and Control

### Public Demand for Control

- **55%** of U.S. adults want more control over how AI is used in their lives
- Both public and experts agree on the importance of personal control

### Confidence in Regulation

- **Public**: 62% have not too much or no confidence in U.S. government to regulate AI effectively
- **Experts**: 53% express similar lack of confidence
- **Trust gap**: This represents significant skepticism across both groups about government regulatory capacity

### Regulatory Preferences

**Gallup parallel findings on regulation:**
- **80%** believe government should maintain rules for AI safety and data security
- **Only 9%** prioritize rapid development over safety measures
- **Cross-party consensus**: 88% of Democrats and 79% of Republicans/Independents support safety rules

## Knowledge and Information

### Public Knowledge Levels

- **64%** report being at least somewhat knowledgeable about AI
- **9%** claim extreme knowledge
- **Gender gap**: Men more informed than women (72% vs. 57%)
- **Age gap**: Significant knowledge drop for those 60+ years old

### Information Gaps

- Limited comprehensive longitudinal tracking of public opinion in the U.S.
- Limiting evidence-based policymaking on AI governance

## Perception of AI Benefits

### What the Public Sees as Potential Benefits

- Scientific research advancement: High support
- Medical applications: Generally positive view
- Productivity improvements: Recognized but concerns about distribution

### Expert Perspective

- Experts see broader positive applications across sectors
- More optimistic about rapid beneficial deployment timelines

## Demographic Variations

### Employment Status

- **AI users** in workplaces: Trust levels higher than general population
- **46%** of active AI users express some level of confidence in AI
- **Non-users**: Only 2% "fully" trust AI systems

### Trust Levels Overall

- **2%** fully trust AI to make unbiased decisions
- **29%** trust it somewhat
- **60%** distrust AI either somewhat (40%) or fully (20%)

## Political Dimension

### Bipartisan Concerns

- Republicans and Democrats share similar concerns about:
  - Job displacement
  - National security impacts
  - Social connections decline
  - Misinformation amplification

### Regulation Support

- Majorities favor multiple oversight approaches:
  - **54%** support government regulation of private companies
  - **53%** prefer industry-created shared standards
  - **Only 16%** believe each company should self-regulate

## International Cooperation Preferences

### Multilateral vs Unilateral Development

- **42%** prefer collaboration with a broad coalition of allies
- **19%** support partnerships with smaller ally coalitions
- **14%** prefer independent U.S. development

### Party Differences

- Democrats show stronger multilateral preference (58%) than Republicans (30%)
- Both groups favor cooperation over isolation

## Independent Testing Standards

- **72%** of Americans want independent experts conducting safety evaluations
- **48%** support government-led testing
- **37%** support company-led testing
