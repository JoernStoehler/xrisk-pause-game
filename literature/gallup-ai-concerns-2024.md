# Gallup Survey: Americans Express Real Concerns About AI (2024)

## Overall Harm vs. Benefit Assessment

### Net Perception of AI

- **56%** believe AI has neutral effects (equal harm and good)
- **31%** think AI does more harm than good (down from 40% in previous year)
- **13%** believe good outweighs harms

This reflects a more balanced view than 2023, with fewer Americans seeing net harm.

## Employment and Economic Concerns

### Job Impact Expectations

- **75%** expect AI will reduce job opportunities in the United States over the next decade
- This represents one of the highest concern areas for the public

### Personal vs. Societal Impact Disconnect

- **28-31%** of British and American adults worry about their own work being automated
- **53-64%** of both populations expect AI to increase overall unemployment
- **Key insight**: People acknowledge broad societal risk while feeling personally insulated

### Job Displacement Responsibility

- **32%** say businesses are mostly responsible for addressing job losses
- **52%** believe government and business share responsibility equally
- **16%** say government bears primary responsibility

## Knowledge and Understanding Levels

### Self-Reported AI Knowledge

- **64%** report being at least somewhat knowledgeable about AI
- **9%** claim extreme or comprehensive knowledge
- **36%** admit limited or no knowledge

### Demographics of Knowledge

**Gender Gap:**
- **72%** of men report being knowledgeable
- **57%** of women report being knowledgeable

**Age Gap:**
- Significant decline in reported knowledge for those 60+ years old
- Younger demographics show higher familiarity

## Concern About Specific AI Applications

### High-Concern Applications (80%+)

- **Hiring decisions**: 85% express concern
- **Autonomous vehicles**: 83% express concern
- **Medical recommendations**: 80% express concern

### Moderate-Concern Applications (50-75%)

- **Political decision-making**: 58% express concern
- **News media coverage**: 53% express concern
- **Student homework assistance**: 67% express concern

### Key Finding

Americans distinguish between AI applications by risk level—the highest concerns cluster around high-stakes decisions that directly affect individuals' lives and safety.

## Trust Issues

### Business Trust in AI Responsibility

- **77%** distrust businesses using AI responsibly
  - **44%** trust "much"
  - **33%** trust "not at all"

### Specific Trust Metrics

- **Only 2%** fully trust AI to make unbiased decisions
- **29%** trust it "somewhat"
- **69%** total distrust (40% somewhat + 29% fully)

### Trust Disparities

Trust levels vary by sector:
- **Finance**: Skepticism especially high
- **Healthcare**: Skepticism especially high
- **Other sensitive industries**: Higher skepticism

## Solutions and Mitigation Strategies

### Transparency as Trust-Builder

- **57%** identify transparency as the most effective strategy businesses can adopt to reduce public concerns
- Suggests that public concerns are not necessarily opposition to AI, but to opaque implementation

### Other Concerns

- **Privacy intrusion**: 72% of Californians concerned (related survey)
- **Misuse for spam/fraud**: 65%
- **Misinformation**: 64%
- **Cybersecurity threats**: 61%

## Demographic Variations

### AI Usage and Concerns

- **35%** of U.S. adults report using AI at least once a week
- **28%** use it less often
- **30%** haven't used AI at all
- **7%** unsure

### By Employment Type

- **73%** of employed Californians believe AI is used in their workplaces
- **Fewer than half** use it daily
- **Only 5%** report career path changes due to AI
- **58%** expect AI will reduce their long-term job opportunities

### Bias Concerns

- **79%** worry that "AI systems might disfavor people from disadvantaged backgrounds or reinforce existing biases"

## Economic Outlook

### Future Economic Expectations

- **50%** expect worsening job markets (California survey)
- **52%** anticipate increased wealth inequality
- **Only 27%** think economy will improve within three years

### Competitiveness View

- **70%** believe AI is important for national and state economic competitiveness
- Yet simultaneously pessimistic about near-term personal/economic impact

**Insight**: Public sees AI as strategically necessary but economically destabilizing.

## Political and Civic Concerns

### Government AI Use Skepticism

- **65%** disapprove of federal government using AI in decisions affecting them
- Gender differences: 8% of women vs. 16% of men support government AI deployment

### Democracy and Information Concerns

- **50%** cannot distinguish real from AI-generated information
- **57%** worry about deepfakes influencing elections

## Worker Protection Support

### Policy Preferences

- **69%** support policies protecting workers from displacement
- **77%** support mandatory AI safety testing by companies
- **79%** support whistleblower protections
- **67%** endorse multistakeholder AI safety efforts (shows rare partisan alignment)

## Cross-Cultural Findings

### International Comparisons

**UK Sentiment (October 2023):**
- **48%** saw AI risks exceeding benefits
- **38%** saw greater benefits

**US Sentiment (August 2023):**
- **52%** felt more concerned than excited

**Emotional Response:**
- Greater nervousness (23-29%) than excitement (16-17%)

## Key Insights for the AI CEO Game

1. **Nuanced Concerns**: Public concern is sophisticated—differentiated by application risk level
2. **Systemic vs. Personal Anxiety**: High awareness of societal risks but belief in personal insulation
3. **Trust Crisis**: Profound gap between AI capability and public confidence in responsible deployment
4. **Transparency Premium**: Public willing to engage with AI if transparency/accountability mechanisms exist
5. **Employment Anxiety**: Job impact is the primary concern—critical for political response in game
6. **Information Integrity Crisis**: Deep concern about AI-generated misinformation and deepfakes affecting elections
